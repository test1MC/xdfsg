Navigating to the Turning Technologies Registration ToolTurning Account Registration Link

All Turning Technologies functions are performed using the Turning Technologies Registration Tool. The Turning Account Registration link directs you to the Turning Account website, where you can download TurningPoint Cloud, view license information and manage your Turning Account.

  1. Log into Blackboard.

  2. Click the Courses tab.
  3. Select the appropriate course below Course List.
  4. Click Tools from the left menu.
  5. Click Turning Technologies Registration ToolTurning Account Registration link.

    The Turning Account website is displayed.

Registering a Participant’s ResponseCard Device ID

The instructor has the option to register a participant’s ResponseCard Device ID. ResponseWare must be registered by the participant.

  1. Locate a participant and click Register ResponseCard Device ID.

  2. Enter the participant’s Device ID and click Register.

  3. Click OK.

Editing a Participant’s ResponseCard Device ID

If a participant entered an incorrect ResponseCard Device ID, the instructor has the option to edit the Device ID. A ResponseWare Device ID cannot be edited.

  1. Select a participant’s Device ID.

  2. Edit the hexadecimal characters and click Update when finished.

    A message appears indicating that the device has been successfully modified.

  3. Click OK.

Downloading a TurningPoint Participant List

Instructors can download a participant list from Blackboard and import it into TurningPoint.

  1. On the main Turning Technologies page, click Download TurningPoint Participant List.
  2. Select Save File and click OK.
  4. Click the Participant List drop-down menu and select Import.
  5. Select TurningPoint Participant List and click Choose File.
  6. Browse to the saved location and click Open.

    The participant list now appears under the Manage tab.

    Use the E-mail Unregistered Students feature to send an e-mail reminder to participants who have not yet registered their devices (ResponseCards and ResponseWare).
