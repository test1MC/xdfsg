Syllabus Recommendations for ResponseWare

Registration: ResponseWare Purchased by the Participant

  • Specify Turning Technologies ResponseWare.
  • Indicate where ResponseWare can be purchased (i.e. bookstore, store.turningtechnologies.com). Participants can purchase a Turning Account license that includes a free ResponseWare license.
  • Participants must create a Turning Account in one of two ways:
    • If using an LMS, sign in to the LMS and follow the Turning Technologies link to the Turning Account page.
    • If no LMS, go to account.turningtechnologies.com.

      In both instances, the student will be routed to the Turning Account website where the student will enter the required information to register their ResponseWare license.

  • Consider including clicker or ResponseWare documentation with your syllabus from www.turningtechnologies.com/user-guides.
  • Identify where to find resources. If your school does not have a participant clicker Help site, consider using turningtechnologies.com/student.
  • If a participant already has a ResponseWare license, he or she will need to update his or her password by visiting responseware.turningtechnologies.com.
  • Participants must visit responseware.turningtechnologies.com to join a session via laptop.
  • Participants can download the ResponseWare app using the Google Play Store for Android or App Store for Apple (if using a web enabled phone/tablet).

Expectations

  • Define how often ResponseWare will be used in class. Most instructors indicate daily use and ask that ResponseWare devices (i.e. phone, laptop, etc.) are brought to every class.
  • Describe responsibility for registration, battery life, getting assistance for problems and bringing the device to class. If you will be making exceptions, make note of them here.
  • Describe how ResponseWare will be used to support participant learning.

    Explain that ResponseWare may be used to gauge understanding of reading material, support class discussions, provide understanding of new concepts and review concepts of previously taught material.

  • Clarify whether or not ResponseWare will be used for formal assessment.
  • Consider if responses should be anonymous depending on the nature of the course.

Attendance, Participation and Grading

  • Identify the role of ResponseWare related to attendance and participation.
  • Describe how you will calculate attendance and participation points.

    A ResponseWare question based on a class reading will be asked at the start of class and will also be used to take attendance. In this case, you may want to consider assigning partial credit for a wrong answer.

  • Specify points or percentage of grade that will be ResponseWare related. In some instances, instructors use ResponseWare points for extra credit.
  • Indicate the dates of specific quizzes or tests that will be administered with ResponseWare.
  • List when grades will be posted.

    Grades will be posted in the LMS within 24 hours of class.

Policy

  • Clearly indicate types of academic misconduct, such as using another participant’s ResponseWare.
  • State your institution's policies and consequences for cheating with ResponseWare.
  • State your personal policies and consequences for cheating with ResponseWare.
