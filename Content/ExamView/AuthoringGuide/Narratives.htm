Narratives

A narrative is a table, descriptive passage, special instruction, picture or other information that can be attached to (or linked to) one or more questions. All questions linked to the same narrative print together as a group on a test, but the instructor is not required to select all of the questions.

You can attach a narrative to any question type except matching. Matching questions cannot be linked to a narrative because the choices already act as a narrative. For figures and tables, label the information and refer to it by that label in a question. See the examples below.

XYZ Company started operations with $40,000 in capital. Expenses are expected to reach $60,000 by the end of the year. Expected revenues are $2,500 per month.

 

1. Based on the information shown above, what is the expected profit or loss for the XYZ Company?

2. Based on the information shown above, what level of revenue is required by the XYZ Corporation to break-even?

3. As shown in Figure 4-1, which button lets you print a test?

4. As shown in Figure 4-1, which button is used to save a test? ANS: Click the button with the floppy disk on it to save a file.

  1. Create a narrative.

    You can create a narrative while you are entering a question, or you can create a narrative while you are viewing the question bank.

    1. Click Narrative while entering a question or click Question from the menu bar and select Narratives.
    2. Click New to display the entry area.
    3. Enter the table or instruction, or insert the image.

      Include a label as part of the narrative. For example, include a label such as Figure 3-2. Then, include the label as part of the question (e.g., As shown in Figure 3- 2, …).

    4. Enter a narrative name in the space provided at the bottom of the entry area.

      Use a descriptive name such as Figure 1-1, Table 1-1, or Process Chart. You must use unique names across the entire project. For example, you can not have a Figure 1 in Chapter 1 and a Figure 1 in Chapter 2. Make the names Figure 1-1 and Figure 2-1. An exception to this rule is when the same exact narrative is used in multiple banks. In this case, use the same narrative name.

      You can include part of the ISBN number with the narrative name (e.g., Figure 1-1 0144-01, Figure 1-2 0144-01) to differentiate among narratives across multiple textbooks.

    5. Click Record to save the narrative.
    6. Click Done when are you finished with the narrative.

  2. Link the questions to the narrative.

    As you are entering or editing the questions, a pop-up menu appears at the bottom of the entry window for all question types except matching. Select the narrative from the list to attach it to the question. When you record the question, the narrative will automatically appear above the question.
